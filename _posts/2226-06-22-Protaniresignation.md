Voltaya district leader Elmer Protani announced his resignation late last night.
He has been facing backlash and pressure to step down from his post after a string of controversal policy decisions and public statements regarding Kolay natives and their uprising against Volt Corp's takeover of Kolaya district.

> "You're going to end up with a beautiful, beautiful building, the way it's supposed to be. Much better than it ever was, actually." -Elmer Protani (A, 

A monolith erupting from the City's skyline, the Assembly building has been standing since the first settlers arrived here. Its sandstone appearance was a welcome change from the brutalist architecture that plagued many of the first megacities, and the rest of Aiatabi district was designed to match. However, 100 years into our great City's life, its age has started to show. 
An initial round of repairs was approved by the VCO when they controlled the Assembly in 2213, and it held for a time. However, as wind speeds over the last 5 years have hit new highs, the damage has increased exponentially.
Critics of the currennt administration were quick to scrutinize the choice of contractor for the repairs. The work was quickly and quietly assigned to Jericho Systems (LTF:H944TH7) who began the process on Thursday of last week. But already, cracks are beginning to form in the re-plastered areas, and the laterite paste used for the patches is changing colors due to constant sun exposure bleaching the pigments. 
Leader Walland said on AVLN that "six people have been detained for the damage they did to our City's now beautiful Assembly building. This was purposefully and criminally done, and somebody had to work very hard, probably in the dark of night, to create such a condition. In any event, even prior to fixing those areas, the building is as beautiful as it can be. We will close down the City center again, either immediately before or after the Founding Day celebrations, to do the permanent repair". The update was posted late last night as reports of increased security around the City center flooded in from Aiatabi residents.
We'll continue to update you when more information is available.
